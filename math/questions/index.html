(Questions 1 and 2 worth one point each, 3 and 4 are two - max three points)

Q1: If n points are collinear, how many different line segments are contained within the first and last points? (example image below: 5 points has 10 line segments -- in other words, find a formula that allows you to determine the number of segments contained within n number of collinear points).

Q2: Six pairs of friends go to a conference. Each person shakes hands with everyone except who they came to the conference with. How many handshakes were exchanged?

Q3: What is the smallest positive integer, n, such that the mth term and the (m+n)th Fibonacci numbers always have the same units digit?

Q4: How many rectangles are in a n x n checkerboard? Determine the number of rectangles in an n x (n-1) checkerboard.
