Office Space

1. What is the role of music in Office Space?

At many points in Office Space, music was used to convey the characters’ emotions and state of minds. Also, for comic effect, the music over-dramatized the scene while also showing characters’ emotions or intent. For example, heavy rap was used during scenes where the main characters were doing “bad things.” The heavy rap also conveyed a feeling of anger and hatred towards work, giving the viewer a sense that the employees had a desire to rebel.

 

2. Are characters’ jobs and response to them essentially similar?

I would say that each main character’s jobs are essentially similar because everyone disliked their job (except for the managers of each business). Every character responded similarly by rebelling in their own way. The employees at the office (Peter and his friends) rebelled by stealing money from the company, Joanna rebelled by flipping her boss off and quitting, and Milton rebelled by burning down the building at the end of the movie.

 

3. Is Office Space more successful than the Unabomber’s Manifesto in making people think about their place in society?

I would say Office Space has a larger chance of making people think about their place in society because of the way it was delivered. While Office Space’s message is delivered via comedy, the Manifesto was delivered through threats and scare tactics. Many people didn’t really listen to the Unabomber’s message but rather were afraid of what he did, how he should be punished, etc. Office Space, on the other hand, although many people would merely treat it as a comedy (a very good one at that) it does have a better chance of making people think of their place in society because people can relate to it better (although it probably exaggerated many things for comedic effect).

 

4. Why did Peter take a new job at the end of the film and seem to have a new lease on life?

After their old company building burned down, the employees went to a different company (at least Peter’s friends at the office did), but Peter went to work at a different non-computerized work place. He did seem to have a new lease on life because he seemed happier and put up with work, possibly even enjoyed it a little. If Peter had gone to work for another computerized he would probably have hated it like his previous job.

 

Revenge effects for a non-computerized work place could be things like advanced construction tools which are supposed to help in construction, but end up complicating matters because the user must learn and know how to operate the tool(s).
