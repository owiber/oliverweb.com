BERKELIUM
Symbol: BK
Density: 14.78 g/cm³
Melting Point: 986°C ± 25°C 
Boiling Point: Unknown
Atomic Number: 97
Atomic Mass: 247
E- Configuration: Rn 7s^2 5f^9
Expected to be a silverish metal.
Half Life: 1400 years

    Berkelium was discovered in December of 1949 by Glenn Seaborg, Stanley G. Thompson, and Albert Ghiorso.  Berkelium was named after the University of California at Berkeley.  Seaborg, Thompson and Albert created Berkelium "by cyclotron bombardment of milligram amounts of 241 Americium with helium ions" (CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics, 1913-1995).  Because Berkelium is rare it sells for $100 per gramand has no known commercial or scientific use.
    The first visible amounts of berkelium compound (berkelium chloride) was produced in 1962 and it weighed 1 billionth of a gram. Berkelium has not yet been prepared in elemental form.
    Berkelium, like other Actinides, kills osteocytes(bone cells), which build bones, therefore it causes skeletal decay.  Berkelium is located in the Innertransition Metals (Actinide series) section of the periodic chart.
